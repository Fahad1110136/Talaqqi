📚 Understanding Tajweed Rules

Before analysis, let's understand what Tajweed means

🎵

Makhaarij al-Huroof

The articulation points of Arabic letters. Know where each sound originates from your vocal apparatus.

Example: ق (Qaf) comes from the deepest part of the throat
🎼

Sifaat al-Huroof

The characteristics of letters including heaviness, lightness, and other qualities.

Example: ظ (Dha) has the characteristic of isti'laa (elevation)
🔄

Rules of Noon & Meem

Ikhfaa, Idghaam, Iqlab, and Idhaar rules for Noon Saakin and Tanween.

Example: منْ بعد → "mim ba'd" (Idghaam with Ghunnah)
⏱️

Madd (Elongation)

Proper elongation of vowels according to their specific counts.

Example: Madd Asli (2 counts), Madd Far'i (2-6 counts)

🎤 Prepare for Your Recording

1

Find a Quiet Place

Choose a room with minimal background noise and echo

2

Use Good Equipment

If possible, use a headset microphone for clearer audio

3

Warm Up Your Voice

Recite a few verses softly to prepare your vocal cords

4

Choose Your Passage

Select a surah you're familiar with (3-5 verses recommended)

📝 Recording Tips:

  • Recite at a moderate pace - not too fast, not too slow
  • Maintain proper breath control between verses
  • Focus on clarity of each letter
  • Pay attention to stops (waqf) and continuations (wasl)
  • Record for 30-60 seconds for best analysis
